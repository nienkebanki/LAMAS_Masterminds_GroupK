Gain vs leak

For each possible guess $g$ we compute:

  • Gain: expected reduction in $|Cand_A(B)|$ after the public announcement.
  • Leak: expected reduction in $|Cand_B(A)|$ after the same announcement.

Trade-off score: $$Score(g)=\alpha\cdot\mathbb{E}[\Delta_B]-\beta\cdot\mathbb{E}[\Delta_A].$$

Concrete strategies

  • Greedy Gain: maximize expected gain.
  • Min-Leak: minimize expected leak.
  • Trade-off: maximize $\alpha$ gain minus $\beta$ leak.
  • HO-Constraint: only allow guesses that preserve a higher-order condition, then maximize trade-off.

Higher-order knowledge

Our HO strategy checks whether $A$ can guarantee that even after the announcement, $B$ still has at least $k$ candidates for $A$: $$K_A(|Cand_B(A)|\ge k).$$ This means $A$ chooses guesses that avoid revealing too much about their own code.
